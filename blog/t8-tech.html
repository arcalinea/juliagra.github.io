Regular Expressions

August 9, 2015


I was amazed when I first learned about regular expressions. Using a brief set of characters, it’s possible to search a string of text for almost anything you want. Regular expressions are powerful tools that help in pattern matching, for example with find and replace operations.

Many programming languages, including Ruby and Javascript, have built-in regular expression capabilities. Others offer their functionality through a library. Each character in a regular expression is either a “metacharacter” with a special meaning that performs an operation, or a regular character that is understood according to its literal meaning.

Here are some of the basic concepts of regular expressions.

    • Boolean “or”
      You can use a vertical bar to separate alternatives. For example, gray | grey matches gray or grey.
      Grouping
      Parentheses are used to group operators. Using the example above, gr( a | e )y also will match gray or grey.
      Quantification
      Quantifying characters specify how often an element is allowed to occur. Some of the most common ones are the question mark, the asterisk, and the plus sign.
      ? indicates zero or one elements
      * indicates zero or more elements
      + indicates one or more elements
